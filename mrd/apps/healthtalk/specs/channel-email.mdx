# Channel D — Email

## Why Email

Many GGz patients — especially older individuals or those in more intensive care — do not actively use WhatsApp but do have email. Email provides a lower-friction fallback and is simpler to implement than WhatsApp (just a link, no interactive mode required).

---

## How It Works

Email delivers a magic link to the patient's registered email address. The link opens the web form (Channel A — `<QuestionnaireForm />`). There is no email-specific interactive mode; the voice option can be triggered from the web form itself.

---

## Email Service

Use a transactional email provider (Postmark recommended for reliability and deliverability). Store credentials as Vercel env vars:

```env
EMAIL_PROVIDER=postmark
POSTMARK_API_KEY=***
EMAIL_FROM=noreply@healthtalk.ai
EMAIL_FROM_NAME=HealthTalk
```

---

## "CALL ME" Reply Handling

A Postmark inbound webhook listens for replies containing "CALL ME" (case-insensitive). On receipt:

1. Vercel identifies the Task from the email thread ID
2. Triggers ElevenLabs outbound call (same as Channel C)
3. Sends confirmation: *"We will call you shortly on [phone number]."*

---

## Email Fallback on WhatsApp Failure

If Twilio reports a WhatsApp delivery failure (webhook status `failed` after retries) and the patient has a registered email address, HealthTalk automatically falls back to email delivery. Task `businessStatus` updated to `email-fallback`.
