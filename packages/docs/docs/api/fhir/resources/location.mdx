# Location

Details and position information for a physical place where services are provided and resources and participants may be stored, found, contained, or accommodated.

  <TabItem value="usage" label="Usage">
    <div>    <a name="scope"></a>   <p> A <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Location</a> includes both incidental locations (a place which is used for healthcare without prior designation or authorization) and  dedicated, formally appointed locations. <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Locations</a> may be private, public, mobile or fixed and scale from small  freezers to full hospital buildings or parking garages. </p> <p>Examples of <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Locations</a> are:</p> <ul> <li>Building, ward, corridor, room or bed</li> <li>Mobile Clinic</li> <li>Freezer, incubator</li> <li>Vehicle or lift</li> <li>Home, shed, or a garage</li> <li>Road, parking place, a park</li> <li>Ambulance (generic)</li> <li>Ambulance (specific)</li> <li>Patient's Home (generic)</li> <li>Jurisdiction</li> </ul>   <p>     These locations are not intended to cover locations on a patient where something occurred     (i.e. a patient's broken leg), but can happily cover the location where the patient broke the leg (the playground)   </p> </div>
    </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="relationships" label="Relationships">
    <div> <a name="bnr"></a>     <p>     <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Locations</a> and <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/organization">Organizations</a> are very closely related resources and can often be mixed/matched/confused.<br/>   The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Location</a> is intended to describe the more physical structures managed/operated by an organization, whereas the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/organization">Organization</a> is intended to   represent the more conceptual hierarchies, such as a ward.  </p>  <p>   A <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Location</a> is valid without an address in cases where it could be purely described by   a geo-coded location in remote areas, or when recorded by a device. <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/location">Locations</a> with a mode = "kind" would   also likely not have an address, as they are just a type of location, but could also have an address where    they can be found at the address.<br/>  </p>  <p>   Another use of location could be for describing a Jurisdiction. This jurisdiction may be considered a classified boundary   which could be a combination of a physical boundary, and some other discriminator(s):  </p>  <ul>   <li>Nation - Country-wide community or Federal Government (Ministry of Health)</li>   <li>Province or State (community or Government)</li>   <li>Business (throughout an enterprise)</li>   <li>Business scope (CDC/FDA)</li>   <li>Business segment (UK Pharmacy)</li>  </ul>  </div>
    </TabItem>
