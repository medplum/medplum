# Claim

A provider issued list of professional services and products which have been provided, or are to be provided, to a patient which is sent to an insurer for reimbursement.

  <TabItem value="usage" label="Usage">
    <div>   <a name="scope"></a>      <p> The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> is used by providers and payors, insurers, to exchange the financial information, and supporting clinical information, regarding the provision  of health care services with payors and for reporting to regulatory bodies and firms which provide data analytics. The primary uses of this resource is to support eClaims,  the exchange of information relating to the proposed or actual provision of healthcare-related goods and services for patients to their benefit payors, insurers and national health programs, for treatment payment planning and reimbursement.   </p>   <p>The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> resource is a "request" resource from a FHIR workflow perspective - see <a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/workflow.html#request">Workflow Request.</a></p>    <p> The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> resource may be interpreted differently depending on its intended use (and the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a>.use element contains the code to indicate):    </p><ul>     <li>claim - where the provision of goods and services is <b>complete</b> and adjudication under a plan and payment is sought.</li>     <li>preauthorization - where the provision of goods and services is <b>proposed</b> and authorization and/or the reservation of funds is desired.</li>     <li>predetermination - where the provision of goods and services is <b>explored</b> to determine what services may be covered and to what amount. Essentially a 'what if' claim.</li>    </ul>    <p></p>    <p> The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> also supports:    </p><ul>     <li>Up to a 3-tier hierarchy of Goods, products, and Services, to support simple to complex billing.</li>     <li>Multiple insurance programs arranged in a Coordination of Benefit sequence to enable exchange with primary, secondary, tertiary etc. insurance coverages.</li>     <li>Assignment of benefit - the benefit may be requested to be directed to the subscriber, the provider or another party.</li>    </ul>   <p></p>   <p>    <b>Mapping to other <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> specifications:</b> Mappings are currently maintained by the Financial Management Work <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/group">Group</a> to UB04 and CMS1500 and are available  at <a href="https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FM/FHIR+Resource+Development">https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FM/FHIR+Resource+Development </a>. Mappings to other specifications may be made available where IP restrictions permit.     </p> <h3 class="self-link-parent"> Additional Information <a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/claim.html#13.6.1.1" title="link to here" class="self-link"></a></h3> <p> Additional information regarding electronic claims content and usage may be found at: </p>   <ul> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#resource-status">Financial Resource Status Lifecycle</a>: how .status is used in the financial resources.</li> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#secondary-use">Secondary Use of Resources</a>: how resources such as <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> and <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/explanationofbenefit">ExplanationOfBenefit</a> may used for    reporting and data exchange for analytics, not just for eClaims exchange between providers and payors.</li> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#subrogation">Subrogation</a>: how eClaims may handle patient insurance coverages when another insurer rather than  the provider will settle the claim and potentially recover costs against specified coverages.</li> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#cob">Coordination of Benefit</a>: how eClaims may handle multiple patient insurance coverages.</li> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#batch">Batches</a>: how eClaims may handle batches of eligibility, claims and responses.</li> <li><a href="https://www.hl7.org/fhir/financial-module.html#attachments">Attachments and Supporting Information</a>: how eClaims may handle the provision of supporting  information, whether provided by content or reference, within the eClaim resource when submitted to the payor or later in a resource which refers  to the subject eClaim resource. This also includes how payors may request additional supporting information from providers.</li> </ul>  </div>

  <TabItem value="relationships" label="Relationships">
    <div>   <a name="bnr"></a>      <p>The <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> resource is used to request the adjudication and/or authorization of a set of healthcare-related goods and services for a patient against the patient's insurance coverages, or  to request what the adjudication would be for a supplied set of goods or services should they be actually supplied to the patient.</p>    <p>When requesting whether the patient's coverage is inforce, whether it is valid at this or a specified date, or requesting the benefit details or preauthorization requirements   associated with a coverage, then <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/coverageeligibilityrequest">CoverageEligibilityRequest</a> should be used instead.</p>    <p>When using the resources for reporting and transferring claims data, which may have originated in some standard other than FHIR, the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> resource is useful if only the  request side of the information exchange is of interest. If, however, both the request and the adjudication information is to be reported then the     <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/explanationofbenefit">ExplanationOfBenefit</a> should be used instead.</p>    <p>For reporting out to patients or transferring data to patient centered applications, such as patient health Record (PHR) application, the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/explanationofbenefit">ExplanationOfBenefit</a>   should be used instead of the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> and <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claimresponse">ClaimResponse</a> resources as those resources may contain provider and payer specific information which is not appropriate for sharing with   the patient.</p>   <p>    <b>The eClaim domain includes a number of related resources</b>   </p>   <table border="1" cellpadding="1" cellspacing="2" frame="box">    <tbody><tr>     <td>Claim</td>     <td>A suite of goods and services and insurances coverages under which adjudication or authorization is requested.</td>    </tr>    <tr>     <td><a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/coverageeligibilityrequest">CoverageEligibilityRequest</a></td>     <td>A request to a payor to: ascertain whether a coverage is in-force at the current or at a specified time; list the table of benefits;     determine whether coverage is provided for specified categories or specific services; and whether preauthorization is required, and if so     what supporting information would be required.</td>    </tr>    <tr>     <td><a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claimresponse">ClaimResponse</a></td>     <td>A payor's adjudication and/or authorization response to the suite of services provided in a <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a>. Typically the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claimresponse">ClaimResponse</a> references the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> but does not duplicate   the clinical or financial information provided in the claim.</td>    </tr>    <tr>     <td><a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/explanationofbenefit">ExplanationOfBenefit</a></td>     <td>This resource combines the information from the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claim">Claim</a> and the <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/claimresponse">ClaimResponse</a>, stripping out any provider or payor proprietary information, into a unified information model    suitable for use for: patient reporting; transferring information to a <a href="/docs/api/fhir/resources/patient">Patient</a> Health Record system; and, supporting complete claim and adjudication information exchange  with regulatory and analytics organizations and other parts of the provider's organization.     </td>    </tr>   </tbody></table>  </div>
