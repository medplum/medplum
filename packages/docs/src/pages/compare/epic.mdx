# Medplum vs Epic

At first glance, Medplum and Epic can appear to solve similar problems. Both support core electronic health record workflows: patient registration, scheduling, charting, labs, prescribing, billing integrations, interoperability, and operational tooling used in real clinical environments.

But in practice, they are designed for very different kinds of healthcare organizations.

Epic is the dominant platform for large hospitals and integrated health systems. It is extraordinarily successful at the problem it was designed to solve: standardizing operations across massive, highly regulated healthcare enterprises with thousands of users, decades of workflows, and deeply interconnected inpatient and outpatient operations.

Medplum is designed for a different category of organization entirely.

Medplum is an open-source healthcare developer platform built for organizations that treat software as a strategic capability rather than a fixed operational appliance. Digital health startups, specialized care networks, virtual-first providers, AI-native healthcare companies, and healthcare infrastructure teams increasingly need systems that are programmable, composable, and deeply customizable. That is the environment Medplum is built for.

This distinction matters because many modern healthcare organizations are not trying to recreate a traditional hospital IT department. They are trying to build differentiated healthcare products, specialized operational workflows, or new care delivery models that move faster than conventional enterprise healthcare software was designed to support.

## Where Epic Excels

Epic is exceptionally strong in large-scale hospital operations.

That includes:

- inpatient workflows
- surgery and perioperative systems
- large multi-site health systems
- complex referral networks
- enterprise revenue cycle operations
- deeply standardized institutional processes
- large clinical and administrative staff organizations

If an organization is operating a major hospital network with thousands of providers and decades of institutional workflow complexity, Epic is often the default choice for good reason.

This is not a market Medplum is attempting to displace.

## Where Medplum Excels

Medplum is optimized for flexibility, programmability, and modern healthcare product development.

Organizations often choose Medplum when they need:

- custom clinical workflows
- embedded healthcare infrastructure
- white-labeled patient or provider experiences
- deep API integrations
- event-driven automation
- AI-native operational tooling
- multi-tenant healthcare platforms
- self-hosting in their own cloud environment
- rapid iteration by engineering teams

Medplum is fully open source under the Apache 2.0 license, with self-hosting supported on Kubernetes, AWS, Azure, and GCP. Rather than exposing a narrow extension surface around a closed application, Medplum exposes the underlying healthcare platform directly: FHIR-native data storage, subscriptions, automation bots, access policies, APIs, authentication infrastructure, and embeddable React components.

That architectural difference becomes increasingly important as healthcare organizations build more custom operational logic and AI-assisted workflows.

## Similarities

Despite the very different architectural philosophies, there is meaningful overlap in core functionality.

The Medplum Provider App includes:

- patient registration
- scheduling
- charting
- e-prescribing integrations
- lab integrations
- billing workflows
- interoperability tooling
- role-based access controls
- audit logging
- FHIR-native APIs

Medplum is also an ONC-certified EHR platform.

For many organizations, the question is not whether Medplum can support clinical workflows. The question is whether they want a fixed enterprise application or a programmable healthcare platform they can adapt and extend themselves.

## Open vs Closed Ecosystems

One useful way to think about the difference is through the lens of software ecosystems.

Epic resembles a highly integrated enterprise operating system. That model offers consistency, standardization, and centralized control — qualities that matter enormously in large hospital environments.

Medplum is closer to the open-source infrastructure model: programmable, composable, and designed to support organizations building their own operational and product differentiation on top of the platform.

That openness creates unusual flexibility.

For example, Hays Medical Center in Kansas used Medplum to build a custom surgery transfer coordination system with cross-site HL7 aggregation and hospital bed management workflows tailored to the operational realities of a rural hospital network. Those kinds of specialized workflows are possible because Medplum exposes the underlying infrastructure directly rather than treating customization as a narrow extension point.

For many organizations, especially startups and specialized care providers, that flexibility is increasingly more valuable than purchasing a monolithic all-in-one system.

## The AI Question

This architectural distinction is becoming more important in the AI era.

Modern healthcare software increasingly depends on:

- event-driven workflows
- agent-assisted operations
- interoperability across fragmented systems
- custom automation
- rapid iteration on operational processes

Those patterns fit naturally on programmable, API-first healthcare infrastructure.

Organizations building AI-native healthcare products often need direct access to the healthcare data model, workflow engine, and automation layer itself — not simply access through a limited customization surface inside a closed enterprise application.

FHIR-native architectures, subscriptions, webhooks, and programmable automation become strategic advantages in that environment.

## Which Organizations Tend to Choose Each?

In broad terms:

Epic is often the right fit for:

- large hospitals
- academic medical centers
- integrated delivery networks
- organizations prioritizing operational standardization at enterprise scale

Medplum is often the right fit for:

- digital health startups
- virtual care platforms
- specialized clinics
- healthcare infrastructure companies
- organizations building custom clinical products
- teams that want engineering ownership of their platform
- organizations pursuing AI-native workflows and automation

These are not mutually exclusive categories. Some organizations use Medplum alongside Epic infrastructure. Others use Medplum to power entirely new operational surfaces that sit outside traditional hospital systems.

## Final Thoughts

From a distance, “Medplum vs Epic” can sound like a comparison between two EHRs.

In practice, it is often a comparison between two very different philosophies of healthcare software.

Epic is one of the most successful enterprise healthcare systems ever built, optimized for large-scale institutional healthcare operations.

Medplum is a programmable healthcare platform optimized for organizations that want to build, customize, and operate modern healthcare software themselves.

For many modern healthcare organizations, the question increasingly is not simply “Which EHR should we buy?” but rather:

“How much control do we want over the healthcare infrastructure our organization depends on?”
